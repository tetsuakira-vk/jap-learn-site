> **Note:** 暑い (*atsui*) means hot weather or the air. 熱い (*atsui*) — same pronunciation — means hot to the touch (food, water, objects). Context tells you which one is meant.

## The Four Seasons 四季

<table class="kanji-table">
<thead><tr><th>Japanese</th><th>Reading</th><th>Romaji</th><th>English</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('はる')">春</span></td><td>はる</td><td>haru</td><td>Spring</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('なつ')">夏</span></td><td>なつ</td><td>natsu</td><td>Summer</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('あき')">秋</span></td><td>あき</td><td>aki</td><td>Autumn / Fall</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('ふゆ')">冬</span></td><td>ふゆ</td><td>fuyu</td><td>Winter</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('つゆ')">梅雨</span></td><td>つゆ</td><td>tsuyu</td><td>Rainy Season</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

> **Culture:** Japan has a rainy season (梅雨 *tsuyu*) from mid-June to mid-July, particularly in Honshu. The word 梅雨 literally means "plum rain" — named for when plums ripen. Typhoon season follows in August–October.
