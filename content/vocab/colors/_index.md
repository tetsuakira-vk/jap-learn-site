Japanese uses native words (和語) for most basic colors. Many also have an adjective form ending in **い** that works directly before nouns — e.g. 赤い車 (*akai kuruma*) — a red car. Click any word to hear it.

> **Tip:** 青 (*ao*) traditionally covers both blue and green. Traffic lights in Japan are called 青信号 even though they appear green — a quirk of history!
