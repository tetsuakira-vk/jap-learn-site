## Free tools worth bookmarking

### Dictionaries
- **[Jisho.org](https://jisho.org)** — The best free English-Japanese dictionary. Search kanji by radical, draw characters, look up words by reading.
- **[Takoboto](https://takoboto.jp)** — Great for quick lookups on mobile.

### Anki (Flashcard app)
The single most powerful tool for memorising kanji and vocabulary. It uses spaced repetition — you only review cards just before you'd forget them.
- [AnkiWeb](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) — Free on desktop and Android, paid on iOS
- Search the shared decks for "Core 2000" or "JLPT N5" to get started

### Kanji study
- **[WaniKani](https://www.wanikani.com)** — Structured kanji learning with stories to help you remember. First 3 levels free.
- **[KanjiDamage](http://www.kanjidamage.com)** — Free, irreverent, and surprisingly effective mnemonics.

### Grammar
- **[Tae Kim's Guide to Japanese](https://guidetojapanese.org)** — The classic free grammar guide. Read this alongside your kana study.
- **[Bunpro](https://bunpro.jp)** — Grammar flashcards with SRS. Great companion to any textbook.

### Immersion
- **[NHK Web Easy](https://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/easy/)** — Real Japanese news written in simple language with furigana. Perfect for intermediate reading practice.
- **[Satori Reader](https://www.satorireader.com)** — Graded Japanese stories with audio. Some content is free.

## Recommended study routine (beginner)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 5 min | Hiragana/Katakana quiz on this site |
| 10 min | Anki flashcards |
| 5 min | Read one NHK Easy article (intermediate) |
| As available | Watch Japanese content (YouTube, anime) |

Consistency beats intensity. 20 minutes every day beats 3 hours on the weekend.

## JLPT levels

If you want a goal to aim for, the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is the standard benchmark:

| Level | Vocabulary | Kanji | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| N5 | ~800 words | ~100 | Absolute beginner |
| N4 | ~1,500 words | ~300 | Beginner |
| N3 | ~3,750 words | ~650 | Intermediate |
| N2 | ~6,000 words | ~1,000 | Upper intermediate |
| N1 | ~10,000+ words | ~2,000 | Advanced / near-native |

Start aiming for N5, then N4. The kanji on this site covers about half the N5 requirements.
