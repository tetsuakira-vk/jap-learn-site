<p class="guide-intro">The JLPT uses the same small set of question types at every level — once you know what each one is asking you to do, none of them will surprise you on test day. This page walks through all of them with a plain-English explanation and a real example you can try.</p>

<div class="guide-at-glance">
  <div><strong>Two scored sections</strong><br>Vocabulary, and Grammar &amp; Reading. This site's mocks cover both. Listening is a third section but requires audio — not included here.</div>
  <div><strong>Always four options</strong><br>Every question is multiple choice with exactly four options, labelled ①②③④. There is no penalty for a wrong answer.</div>
  <div><strong>Scaled scoring</strong><br>Raw correct answers are converted to a 0–60 scaled score per section. You must hit 19 points in <em>every</em> section, not just the overall total.</div>
  <div><strong>No skipping sections</strong><br>A strong Grammar score cannot compensate for a weak Vocabulary score. Both sections must pass the 19-point minimum independently.</div>
</div>

<div class="guide-section-hd">Section 1 — Language Knowledge: Vocabulary 〔言語知識（文字・語彙）〕</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題１</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Reading kanji <span>漢字読み</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A sentence with one word underlined. That word is written in kanji. Your job is to pick the correct furigana reading from four options. The wrong options are plausible mis-readings — similar sounds or different on/kun readings of the same characters. Read the sentence for context; sometimes it helps narrow down the meaning even if you're unsure of the reading.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">毎日、日本語を<span class="mock-hl">勉強</span>しています。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex1" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">① べんきょう</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex1" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">② ぶんきょう</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex1" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">③ べんけい</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex1" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">④ ぶんけい</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題２</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Writing kanji <span>漢字表記</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">N5 and N4 only</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">The reverse of 問題１. An underlined word is written in hiragana — you pick the correct kanji spelling from four options. The wrong options use characters that look similar or share the same reading but have a different meaning. This question type only appears at N5 and N4; from N3 upwards it is replaced by additional vocabulary questions.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">ここから<span class="mock-hl">えき</span>まで歩いて10分かかります。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex2" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">① 遅</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex2" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">② 駅</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex2" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">③ 役</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex2" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">④ 訳</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題３</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Vocabulary in context <span>文脈規定</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A sentence with a gap — （　） — and four vocabulary words to choose from. Only one fits the meaning of the sentence. The trap options are often words that sound related or appear in the same topic area, so you need to think about the meaning of the whole sentence, not just the gap in isolation.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">のどがかわいたので、水を（　）。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex3" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">① たべます</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex3" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">② のみます</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex3" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">③ よみます</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex3" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">④ かいます</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題４</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Synonyms &amp; paraphrase <span>言い換え類義語</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A sentence with one word or phrase underlined. You choose the option that is closest in meaning to the underlined part — either a single synonym or a paraphrased sentence. This tests whether you genuinely understand the word, not just recognise it. The wrong options are often words or phrases that seem related but shift the meaning in a specific way.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">「この電車は<span class="mock-hl">まもなく</span>出発します。」</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex4" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">① もうすぐ</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex4" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">② ちょうど</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex4" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">③ ずっと</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex4" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">④ まだ</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題５</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Word usage <span>用法</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A sentence with a gap, where you select the word that fits correctly — similar to 問題３, but at higher levels the options are often grammatically interchangeable and the difference is purely about which word collocates naturally with the surrounding sentence. Knowing which words tend to appear together in Japanese is the key skill here.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">田中さんはいつも私が困っているときに（　）てくれます。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex5" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">① 助け</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex5" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">② 知り</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex5" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">③ 待ち</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex5" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">④ 思い</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-section-hd">Section 2 — Grammar and Reading 〔言語知識（文法）・読解〕</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題１</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Grammar fill-in <span>文の文法１</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A sentence with a gap. You choose the correct grammar form, particle, or conjugation. At lower levels this is often a particle (で、に、を) or a simple verb ending. At higher levels the options are closely related grammar patterns where the nuance between them is the real test — for example choosing between ために and ように, or between たら and なら.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">毎日、電車（　）会社へ行きます。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex6" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,2)">① が</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex6" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,2)">② に</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex6" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,2)">③ で</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex6" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,2)">④ を</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題２</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Sentence ordering <span>文の文法２</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">This is the most distinctive JLPT question type. You're given an incomplete sentence with four blank slots. Four word or phrase chunks fill those slots — but you're only asked which chunk belongs in the slot marked ★. To find it, you need to mentally assemble the whole sentence in the correct order, then identify what lands on the star. Read all four chunks first, build the sentence, and work out the ★ position from there.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it — build the sentence, then pick what goes in the ★ slot</div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-ordering-sentence">
      彼女は&nbsp;
      <span class="mq-blank-slot">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span>
      <span class="mq-blank-slot mq-star-slot">★</span>
      <span class="mq-blank-slot">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span>
      上手です。
    </p>
    <p class="mq-chunks">① が　　② 日本語　　③ とても</p>
    <p class="mq-ordering-q">★の位置に入るものはどれですか。 / Which chunk goes in the ★ position?</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex7" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">① が</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex7" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">② 日本語</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex7" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">③ とても</label>
    </div>
    <p style="font-size:0.78rem;color:#3a4870;margin-top:0.8rem">Correct order: 彼女は ③とても ②日本語 ①が 上手です。 → ★ = ②</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題３</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Passage grammar <span>文章の文法</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A short passage (usually 5–8 sentences) with five numbered gaps. Each gap has four options. Unlike 問題１ where each sentence stands alone, here the gaps are connected — the tense, topic, and flow of the whole passage constrain what fits in each slot. Read the whole passage first before answering, and check that your choices are consistent with each other.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it — the passage has one gap shown here</div>
  <div class="mock-passage">
  先週の日曜日、友達と一緒に山に行きました。朝6時に家を出て、電車で2時間かかりました。山の頂上からの景色はとても<strong>（　）</strong>。お昼ご飯はみんなで持ってきたお弁当を食べました。
  </div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">（　）に入る最もよいものはどれですか。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex8" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">① きれいでした</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex8" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">② きれいです</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex8" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">③ きれいだ</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex8" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,0)">④ きれいな</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題４</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Reading comprehension <span>内容理解</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A passage followed by two or three comprehension questions. The questions ask about facts stated in the text, the writer's intention, or what a passage implies. The key trap is choosing an option that sounds reasonable but contradicts or goes beyond what the text actually says. Stick to what the passage tells you — don't add outside knowledge.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mock-passage">
  山田さんは今年の４月から新しい会社で働いています。最初は仕事が難しくて毎日疲れていましたが、会社の先輩が丁寧に教えてくれたので、だんだん仕事に慣れてきました。今では仕事が楽しいと感じています。
  </div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">山田さんが仕事に慣れてきた理由は何ですか。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex9" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">① 仕事が簡単だったから</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex9" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">② 先輩が丁寧に教えてくれたから</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex9" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">③ 会社が近かったから</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex9" value="3" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">④ 友達が同じ会社にいたから</label>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="guide-qtype">
  <div class="guide-qtype-hd">
    <span class="guide-qtype-num">問題５</span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-name">Information retrieval <span>情報検索</span></span>
    <span class="guide-qtype-badge">All levels</span>
  </div>
  <p class="guide-qtype-desc">A practical document — a notice, timetable, email, flyer, or sign — followed by two questions. The questions ask you to extract specific information: a date, a price, a condition, a rule. You don't need to read the whole document in depth; scan for the relevant section. The wrong options often use numbers or words that appear in the document but in a different context.</p>
  <div class="guide-example-label">Try it</div>
  <div class="mock-passage mock-notice">
  <strong>日本語クラス　生徒募集</strong><br><br>
  毎週火曜日・木曜日<br>
  時間：午後7時〜9時<br>
  場所：市民センター3階<br>
  月謝：2,000円<br>
  定員：20名（先着順）<br>
  お申し込み：tanaka@example.com
  </div>
  <div class="mq">
    <p class="mq-text">このクラスは１週間に何回ありますか。</p>
    <div class="mq-opts">
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex10" value="0" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">① 1回</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex10" value="1" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">② 2回</label>
      <label><input type="radio" name="ex10" value="2" onchange="checkEx(this,1)">③ 3回</label>

function checkEx(radio, correct) {
  var q = radio.closest('.mq');
  if (q.classList.contains('mq-right') || q.classList.contains('mq-wrong')) return;
  var chosen = parseInt(radio.value);
  q.querySelectorAll('.mq-opts label').forEach(function(lbl, i) {
    if (i === correct) lbl.classList.add('mq-correct-lbl');
    else if (i === chosen) lbl.classList.add('mq-wrong-lbl');
  });
  q.querySelectorAll('input').forEach(function(inp) { inp.disabled = true; });
  q.classList.add(chosen === correct ? 'mq-right' : 'mq-wrong');
}
</script>
