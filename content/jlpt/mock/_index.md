### About the JLPT

The **Japanese Language Proficiency Test** (日本語能力試験) is the world's most widely used Japanese language certification, administered twice a year (July & December) by the Japan Foundation. There are five levels: N5 (beginner) to N1 (advanced).

Each level tests **vocabulary**, **grammar**, **reading**, and **listening**. The mock tests here cover the first three sections — listening requires audio, which you can find in the official JLPT practice workbooks (公式問題集).

| Level | Vocabulary | Grammar | Reading | Listening | Pass mark |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| N5 | ~800 words | Basic patterns | Short texts | Simple | 80/180 |
| N4 | ~1,500 words | Everyday patterns | Medium texts | Everyday | 90/180 |
| N3 | ~3,750 words | Various patterns | Various texts | Various | 95/180 |
| N2 | ~6,000 words | Complex patterns | Complex texts | Complex | 90/180 |
| N1 | ~10,000 words | Advanced patterns | Sophisticated | Advanced | 100/180 |
