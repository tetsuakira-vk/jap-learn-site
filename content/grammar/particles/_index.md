Particles are small words attached after nouns, verbs, and adjectives. They show how each word relates to the rest of the sentence. Getting particles right is the single biggest grammar step in Japanese.

Click any Japanese sentence to hear it.

---

## は — Topic Marker

**Function:** Marks what the sentence is *about* — the topic. Does not always mark who does the action.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('私は学生です。')">私は学生です。</span> | watashi wa gakusei desu | I am a student. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('今日は暑いですね。')">今日は暑いですね。</span> | kyou wa atsui desu ne | It's hot today, isn't it. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('これは本ですか？')">これは本ですか？</span> | kore wa hon desu ka | Is this a book? |

> **は vs が:** は marks the *topic*; が marks the *subject* (who actually does the action). 「**私が**食べた」 = *I* was the one who ate. 「**私は**食べた」 = As for me, I ate.

---

## が — Subject Marker

**Function:** Marks the grammatical subject — who does the action or who/what is being described. Also used for ability, desire, and preference.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('猫が寝ています。')">猫が寝ています。</span> | neko ga nete imasu | The cat is sleeping. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語が好きです。')">日本語が好きです。</span> | nihongo ga suki desu | I like Japanese. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語が話せます。')">日本語が話せます。</span> | nihongo ga hanasemasu | I can speak Japanese. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('どこが痛いですか？')">どこが痛いですか？</span> | doko ga itai desu ka | Where does it hurt? |

> **が for likes, abilities, and desires:** Japanese uses が (not を) for the object of 好き (like), 嫌い (dislike), できる (can do), ほしい (want), わかる (understand).

---

## を — Object Marker

**Function:** Marks the direct object — what the verb acts on. Also marks the route you move through.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('寿司を食べます。')">寿司を食べます。</span> | sushi wo tabemasu | I eat sushi. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('本を読んでいます。')">本を読んでいます。</span> | hon wo yonde imasu | I'm reading a book. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('音楽を聞きます。')">音楽を聞きます。</span> | ongaku wo kikimasu | I listen to music. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('公園を散歩します。')">公園を散歩します。</span> | kouen wo sanpo shimasu | I take a walk through the park. |

---

## に — Direction / Time / Location of Existence

**Function:** Covers several related uses — destination of movement, point in time, indirect object (recipient), and location where something exists.

| Use | Japanese | Reading | English |
|----|----------|---------|---------|
| Destination | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本に行きます。')">日本に行きます。</span> | nihon ni ikimasu | I'm going to Japan. |
| Time | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('三時に起きます。')">三時に起きます。</span> | sanji ni okimasu | I wake up at 3 o'clock. |
| Recipient | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('友達にプレゼントをあげます。')">友達にプレゼントをあげます。</span> | tomodachi ni purezento wo agemasu | I give a present to my friend. |
| Existence | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('駅の前に銀行があります。')">駅の前に銀行があります。</span> | eki no mae ni ginkou ga arimasu | There's a bank in front of the station. |
| Purpose | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('買い物に行きます。')">買い物に行きます。</span> | kaimono ni ikimasu | I'm going (to do some) shopping. |

> **に vs で (location):** に marks *where something exists* (います / あります). で marks *where an action happens*.
> 「図書館**に**本があります」= The book is *at* the library. 「図書館**で**勉強します」= I study *at* the library.

---

## で — Location of Action / Means / Reason

**Function:** Where an action takes place, the means/tool used, or the reason/cause.

| Use | Japanese | Reading | English |
|----|----------|---------|---------|
| Location of action | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('学校で日本語を勉強します。')">学校で日本語を勉強します。</span> | gakkou de nihongo wo benkyou shimasu | I study Japanese at school. |
| Means / tool | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('電車で行きます。')">電車で行きます。</span> | densha de ikimasu | I go by train. |
| Language / means | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語で話してください。')">日本語で話してください。</span> | nihongo de hanashite kudasai | Please speak in Japanese. |
| Reason | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('病気で学校を休みました。')">病気で学校を休みました。</span> | byouki de gakkou wo yasumimashita | I was absent from school due to illness. |

---

## へ — Direction (Toward)

**Function:** Indicates direction of movement — similar to に for destinations but emphasises the journey rather than arrival. Often interchangeable with に.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('東京へ行きます。')">東京へ行きます。</span> | toukyou e ikimasu | I'm going to / toward Tokyo. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('こちらへどうぞ。')">こちらへどうぞ。</span> | kochira e douzo | This way, please. |

---

## と — With / And (exhaustive)

**Function:** Lists things together (exhaustively — "A and B and nothing else"), or marks who you do something *with*.

| Use | Japanese | Reading | English |
|----|----------|---------|---------|
| Together with | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('友達と映画を見ました。')">友達と映画を見ました。</span> | tomodachi to eiga wo mimashita | I watched a film with a friend. |
| List (and) | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('コーヒーと紅茶があります。')">コーヒーと紅茶があります。</span> | koohii to koucha ga arimasu | There is coffee and tea. |
| Quoting | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('「大丈夫」と言いました。')">「大丈夫」と言いました。</span> | "daijoubu" to iimashita | They said "it's fine." |

> **と vs や:** と lists things *exhaustively* (only those items). や lists things *partially* (among others): 「コーヒー**や**紅茶」= coffee and tea (and other things).

---

## も — Also / Even / Too

**Function:** Replaces は or が/を to mean "also" or "too". With numbers it means "as many/much as" (emphasises unexpectedly large amount).

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('私も行きます。')">私も行きます。</span> | watashi mo ikimasu | I'm going too. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語も中国語も話せます。')">日本語も中国語も話せます。</span> | nihongo mo chuugokugo mo hanasemasu | I can speak both Japanese and Chinese. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('百人も来ました。')">百人も来ました。</span> | hyakunin mo kimashita | As many as a hundred people came! |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('何もありません。')">何もありません。</span> | nani mo arimasen | There is nothing at all. |

---

## の — Possession / Connection / Nominalisation

**Function:** Connects nouns (like 's in English), links descriptions to nouns, and turns verbs/adjectives into noun phrases.

| Use | Japanese | Reading | English |
|----|----------|---------|---------|
| Possession | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('私の本です。')">私の本です。</span> | watashi no hon desu | It's my book. |
| Connection | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語の先生です。')">日本語の先生です。</span> | nihongo no sensei desu | A Japanese-language teacher. |
| Nominaliser | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語を話すのは難しいです。')">日本語を話すのは難しいです。</span> | nihongo wo hanasu no wa muzukashii desu | Speaking Japanese is difficult. |
| Explanatory | <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('どうしたんですか？')">どうしたんですか？</span> | doushita n desu ka | What happened? (seeking explanation) |

---

## か — Question Marker

**Function:** Added to the end of any sentence to make it a question. No question mark needed in formal writing.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('これは何ですか？')">これは何ですか？</span> | kore wa nan desu ka | What is this? |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語が話せますか？')">日本語が話せますか？</span> | nihongo ga hanasemasu ka | Can you speak Japanese? |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('コーヒーか紅茶はいかがですか？')">コーヒーか紅茶はいかがですか？</span> | koohii ka koucha wa ikaga desu ka | Would you like coffee or tea? |

> **か also means "or"** when placed between options: 「コーヒー**か**紅茶」= coffee or tea.

---

## から / まで — From / Until

**Function:** から marks starting point in time or space ("from"). まで marks the end point ("until", "as far as").

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('九時から始まります。')">九時から始まります。</span> | kuji kara hajimarimasu | It starts from 9 o'clock. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('駅から歩いて五分です。')">駅から歩いて五分です。</span> | eki kara aruite gofun desu | It's a 5-minute walk from the station. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('五時まで働きます。')">五時まで働きます。</span> | goji made hatarakimasu | I work until 5 o'clock. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('月曜日から金曜日まで学校があります。')">月曜日から金曜日まで学校があります。</span> | getsuyoubi kara kin'youbi made gakkou ga arimasu | School is Monday through Friday. |

> から also means **"because"** at the end of a clause: 「疲れた**から**、寝ます」= I'm going to sleep *because* I'm tired.

---

## より — Than (Comparison)

**Function:** Marks the standard of comparison — what you're comparing *against*.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('東京より大阪の方が好きです。')">東京より大阪の方が好きです。</span> | toukyou yori oosaka no hou ga suki desu | I like Osaka more than Tokyo. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('昨日より今日の方が寒いです。')">昨日より今日の方が寒いです。</span> | kinou yori kyou no hou ga samui desu | Today is colder than yesterday. |

> **Full comparison structure:** A は B **より** [adjective] です。= A is [adj] *than* B.

---

## だけ — Only / Just

**Function:** Limits to one thing — "only" or "just". Similar to しか but positive.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('少しだけ分かります。')">少しだけ分かります。</span> | sukoshi dake wakarimasu | I understand just a little. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('一つだけください。')">一つだけください。</span> | hitotsu dake kudasai | Just one, please. |

---

## しか〜ない — Only (with negative)

**Function:** "Nothing but / only" — always used with a negative verb. Stronger and more restrictive than だけ.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('百円しかありません。')">百円しかありません。</span> | hyakuen shika arimasen | I only have 100 yen. (lit. I have nothing but 100 yen) |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('日本語しか話せません。')">日本語しか話せません。</span> | nihongo shika hanasemasen | I can only speak Japanese. |

> **だけ vs しか〜ない:** 「百円**だけ**あります」= I have only 100 yen (neutral). 「百円**しか**ありません」= I have only 100 yen (implies it's not enough).

---

## ね — Seeking Agreement

**Function:** Added at the end to seek the listener's agreement or share a feeling — like "isn't it?" or "right?". Softens statements.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('今日は寒いですね。')">今日は寒いですね。</span> | kyou wa samui desu ne | It's cold today, isn't it. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('そうですね。')">そうですね。</span> | sou desu ne | That's right. / I agree. |

---

## よ — Assertion / New Information

**Function:** Added at the end to assert something the listener may not know, or to emphasise a point. Stronger than ね — you're telling the listener something.

| Japanese | Reading | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('これはおいしいですよ。')">これはおいしいですよ。</span> | kore wa oishii desu yo | This is delicious, I'm telling you. |
| <span class="pt-speak" onclick="speak('大丈夫ですよ。')">大丈夫ですよ。</span> | daijoubu desu yo | It's fine, don't worry. |

> **ね vs よ:** ね shares a feeling with the listener (you expect agreement). よ tells the listener something new (you have information they don't).
