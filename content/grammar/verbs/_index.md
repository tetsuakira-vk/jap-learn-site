Japanese verbs fall into three groups. Once you know the group, every form follows the same rule. Click any Japanese to hear it.

---

## The Three Verb Groups

| Group | Also called | How to identify | Example |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Group 1** | う-verbs / godan | Ends in any う-sound except -iru/-eru | 書く, 飲む, 話す |
| **Group 2** | る-verbs / ichidan | Ends in -iru or -eru | 食べる, 見る, 起きる |
| **Irregular** | — | Just two: する and くる | する, くる |

> **Shortcut:** If a verb ends in -iru or -eru, it's *probably* Group 2. But watch out for exceptions — 切る (kiru, to cut) ends in -iru but is Group 1.

### て-form rules for Group 1

The て-form is one of the most-used forms — it connects clauses, makes requests, and builds compound tenses. The ending changes based on the verb's final consonant:

| Verb ending | て-form | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| く → いて | 書く → 書いて | iku → itte *(exception: 行く → 行って)* |
| ぐ → いで | 泳ぐ → 泳いで | oyogu → oyoide |
| す → して | 話す → 話して | hanasu → hanashite |
| ぬ / む / ぶ → んで | 飲む → 飲んで | nomu → nonde |
| う / つ / る → って | 買う → 買って | kau → katte |

> **Compound する verbs:** Any noun + する creates a verb. 勉強 (study) → 勉強する. 練習 (practice) → 練習する. These all conjugate like する.

## Adjective Forms

Japanese has two adjective types, each with its own conjugation.

### い-adjectives

| Form | 高い (takai, expensive/tall) |
|------|------------------------------|
| Present | <span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('高いです')">高いです</span> — takai desu |
| Negative | <span class="kt-char" onclick="speak('高くないです')">高くないです</span> — takaku nai desu |
